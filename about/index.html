Jose Barba-Montoya

I am a Research Associate in the Division of Invertebrate Zoology at the American Museum of Natural History, collaborating with Professor Lorenzo Prendini and the Arachnology Lab to investigate evolutionary patterns across the arachnid tree of life through phylogenomics.

Background

2017: Ph.D. in Molecular Evolution, Department of Genetics, Evolution and Environment, University College London (UCL), UK. Bayesian molecular clock dating and the divergence times of angiosperms and primates. Supervised by Professor Ziheng Yang.

2012: M.Sc. in Systematics, Institute of Biology, National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), Mexico City, Mexico. Diversification of Pachycereeae (Cactaceae) due to pollination syndromes. Supervised by Professor Susana Magallón.

2009: B.Sc. in Biology, Faculty of Sciences, UNAM, Mexico City, Mexico. Origin and evolution of tribe Pachycereeae (Cactaceae). Supervised by Professor Susana Magallón.

Professional and Research Experience

Nov 2025 – date: Research Associate in the Division of Invertebrate Zoology at the American Museum of Natural History (AMNH), New York, NY, USA.

Nov 2023 – Nov 2025: Gerstner Scholar in Bioinformatics and Computational Biology at the Richard Gilder Graduate School (RGGS), AMNH, New York, NY, USA. Collaborated with Professor Lorenzo Prendini to study arachnid evolution using phylogenomic methods. Also involved in teaching and training in comparative genomics, programming (Bash, R, Python), and high-performance computing.

Sep 2018 – Oct 2023: Postdoctoral Researcher in the Laboratory of Professor Sudhir Kumar at Institute for Genomics and Evolutionary Medicine, Temple University, Philadelphia, PA, USA. Research focused on developing, evaluating, and implementing models and methods for phylogenomic analysis and species divergence time estimation, as well as conducting large-scale data analyses.

Jan – Sep 2018: Research and consulting for Agroproductos de Alto Valor, Mexico City, Mexico. Research on plant species cultivated in Mexico with high nutritional value for production of tortilla flours.

Oct – Dec 2017: Tree Planting Supervisor (volunteer) at Trees for Cities, London, UK. Supervision and support to the community people to plant trees.

Jan – Sep 2012: Research Assistant for Professor Susana Magallón at Intstitute of Biology, UNAM, Mexico City, Mexico. Collection of wild specimens, DNA extraction, PCR amplification and sequencing of succulent plants.

Feb – July 2012: Researcher at Papalote Children’s Museum, Mexico City, Mexico. Research on natural history and biology of Brossimum alicastrum.

Dec 2010: Visiting Researcher, Institute of Ecology, UNAM, Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico. Research on cactus pollination and collection of Cactaceae and Fouquieriaceae wild specimens.

Apr – May 2010: Visiting Researcher, Dessert Botanical Garden, Phoenix, AZ, USA. Collection of Cactaceae specimens from the botanical garden and preparation of herbarium specimens.

Jan – March 2009: Researcher, Verde Vertical, Mexico City, Mexico. Development of sustainable hydroponics.
